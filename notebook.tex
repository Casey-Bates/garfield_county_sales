    The plot above is a boxplot. The box represents the middle 50\% of
values, which spans the 25th percentile to the 75th percentile. Read
more about boxplots
\textbf{\href{https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability/summarizing-quantitative-data/box-whisker-plots/a/box-plot-review}{here}}.

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  A wider box indicates greater variability in the data (in this case,
  Carbondale has a wide range of home sizes, but the homes in Parachute
  are smaller and have less variability in size)
\item
  The dots above the ``whisker'' represent ``outliers'' (really big or
  really small homes)
\item
  The line in the middle of the box represents the median (the middle
  value) which has an equal number of points above and below it.
\item
  The median is less affected by outliers than the mean
\end{itemize}
